About Beehive Of AI

A distributed AI processing platform that's fault-tolerant by design — because the hive survives even when individual bees don't.

🎯 The Problem We're Solving

Existing solutions fall short

Distributed AI projects like Petals and EXO try to split model layers across computers. This means if any single machine goes offline, the entire task fails — and every node needs to be online simultaneously.

Our approach: task parallelism

Beehive Of AI splits the work, not the model. Each Worker Bee runs a complete small model locally. If a worker drops out, another picks up the sub-task. The Queen Bee's larger model handles coordination.

🐝

Worker Bee

Has a home computer with a GPU. Runs a local Ollama model. Joins a Hive, waits for sub-tasks, processes them, and earns money. Can be offline sometimes — the Hive keeps working without them.

👑

Queen Bee

Runs a larger AI model (the Queen model). Receives full tasks from Beekeepers, splits them into sub-tasks, assigns them to Workers, collects results, and combines them into the final answer.

🏢

Beekeeper

A company or individual who submits AI tasks (Nectar) and receives results (Honey). Pays per job. Doesn't need to know anything about AI — just submit your question and get an answer.
